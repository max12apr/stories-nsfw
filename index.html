A Room With Closed Curtains

The hotel room was small, meant for passing nights and unspoken truths. Heavy curtains shut the world out, leaving only lamplight and the quiet tension between two people who had arrived separately, carefully, carrying lives that could not touch this moment.

They spoke of ordinary things to hide the extraordinary pull between them, of weather and delays and routines waiting elsewhere. Silence kept returning, thick with everything they were not supposed to feel.

In that dim room, time felt paused, as if the world had agreed not to look too closely. They did not make promises. They shared only the understanding that some connections exist in secret, carried carefully, like a flame that must never be seen in daylight.
